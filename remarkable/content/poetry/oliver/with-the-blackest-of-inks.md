At night
the panther,
who is lean
and quick,

is only
a pair of eyes
and, with a yawn,
momentarily,

a long, pink tongue.
Mostly
he listens
as he walks

on the puffs
of his feet
as if
on a carpet

from Persia,
or leaps
into the branches
of a tree,

or swims
across the river,
or simply
stands in the grass

and waits.
Because, Sir,
you have given him,
for your own reasons,

everything that he needs:
leaves, food, shelter;
a conscience
that never blinks.

---

Mary Oliver
